June 2028 — Citrini Doomer Scenario

AI agents became invisible infrastructure. S&P down 38%. Unemployment 10.2%.
"People were using AI agents who didn't even know what an AI agent was."

Scenario Rationale AI becomes invisible plumbing. Citrini describes a world where LLM usage became default by early 2027 — embedded in phones, running as background agents, handling shopping, insurance, travel. The median American consumed 400K tokens/day by March 2027. The "free user" category explodes because AI is simply what your phone does now. But the paid tier is suppressed — mass white-collar unemployment means fewer individuals can justify $20/mo, even as businesses spend heavily on AI to replace headcount. Coding scaffolds grow ~20x but remain niche; the article notes AI "writes essentially all code" but that's corporate AI, not individual subscriptions. The developing world still lags, keeping ~35% of humanity AI-untouched.

June 2028 — Opus 4.6 Projection

A more measured view. Rapid but uneven adoption. Economic disruption is real but not catastrophic.
The key difference: a healthier economy means more people choose to pay.
Never used AI · ~4.1B (50%)
Free chatbot user · ~2.9B (35%)
Pays $20/mo for AI · ~900M (11%)
Uses coding scaffold · ~330M (4%)
Projection Rationale Faster paid adoption, slower passive saturation. My projection diverges from Citrini's in structure, not total AI contact. I expect AI capabilities to advance rapidly, but the economic feedback loops to be slower and less catastrophic than the doomer scenario models — policy response, corporate inertia, and regulatory friction all act as brakes. The critical difference: in a healthier economy, more people can afford and choose to pay for premium AI tools. Coding scaffolds see the largest relative growth (~80x vs. today) as AI-assisted development becomes a standard professional skill, not just an engineering tool. The developing world adopts more slowly — connectivity, language support, and economic incentives keep ~50% of humanity outside AI's direct reach by mid-2028. The total "AI touched" population is similar to Citrini's (~50% vs ~65%), but the distribution skews toward higher-value engagement.
